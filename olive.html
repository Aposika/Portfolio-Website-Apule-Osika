Olive

Overview

Purpose

Many individuals have busy jobs and no time for “work-life balance.” The Olive app aims to make staying on top of health much easier by providing a one-stop shop for individual's medical, health, and wellness needs.

Objective

My challenge was to design Olive features that improved user access to a centralized area for health and wellness resources as well as medical information.

Role

UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer

Duration

10 months (May 2022- March 2023)

Tools

  • Figma
  • Canva
  • Microsoft Office Suite
  • Zoom

Process

User Research

I performed user interviews to gain insights into what my end users want, need, and their behaviors. From my exploratory research, three User Personas emerged.

I chose to make Olivia my primary persona for the Olive project due to fact that mental health needs are a large burden of illness in youth today, and because this problem could be handled more successfully through apps like Olive.

About Olivia

Olivia's Journey map showcases Olivia's potential thoughts, emotions, wants and needs while completing a task within the Olive app. Based on her scenario, Olivia aims to complete the task of logging and storing a medication in the app. Below are the steps she needs to follow to successfully complete this task.

This is an example of one of Olivia's designed user flows. It shows the pages she will interact with in order to log and store a medication in Olive.

Information Architecture

Before moving on to the prototyping stage of the Olive project, I conducted cardsorting, which was the first iteration of the Olive project, and created a sitemap that laid out the information architecture and structure of the Olive app.

Prototyping

Next, I designed low, mid, and high fidelity prototypes that followed the sitemap structure and that followed Olivia's three designed user flows. These three user flows were pertinent to Olivia's mental health needs, and are the three main features of the Olive app.

Usability Testing

The second iteration of the Olive project came in the form of six usability tests via Zoom calls. All participants were able to complete the three tasks I requested. I was able to highlight several major and minor errors that will be taken into account for future iterations of the Olive product.

I used affinity mapping to organize the results of the usability tests.

View Affinity Map

User Interface (UI)

The third iteration of the Olive project was design critiques of the User Interface (UI) of the Olive high fidelity prototypes via peer feedback

Accessibility

The fourth and final iteration of the Olive project was designing for accessibility.
